Alberobello is a picturesque town located in the southern region of Apulia, Italy. It is renowned for its unique and iconic trulli, which are traditional dry-stone huts with conical roofs. These whimsical buildings give Alberobello a distinct and enchanting appearance, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can wander through the charming streets of the town, exploring the trulli, many of which have been converted into shops, cafes, and accommodations. Alberobello offers a glimpse into the region's rich history and cultural heritage, and it has become a popular tourist destination known for its exceptional architecture and historical significance.

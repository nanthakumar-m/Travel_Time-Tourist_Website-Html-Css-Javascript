Lecce is a charming and historic city located in the southern region of Italy, known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning Baroque architecture, and warm Mediterranean climate. Often referred to as the "Florence of the South," Lecce boasts a wealth of ornate buildings, churches, and palaces that showcase the unique Lecce Baroque architectural style. The city's historic center is a labyrinth of narrow cobblestone streets and picturesque squares, with the Basilica di Santa Croce and the Piazza del Duomo standing out as prime examples of this intricate and exuberant architectural tradition. Lecce is also celebrated for its vibrant street life, bustling piazzas, and an abundance of local restaurants serving delicious Salento cuisine, making it a delightful destination for both history enthusiasts and food lovers.
